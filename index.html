faq

Questions, answered

What is ArchBTL?

ArchBTL is a developer tool that compresses infrastructure logs so AI agents can read them. It cuts a huge log window down to the few lines that matter, schema-valid, in seconds, so your agent gets the same answer at a fraction of the tokens.

How is ArchBTL different from raw logs or Drain3?

ArchBTL keeps every line that matters and tags it by its role in the incident, with a pointer to the real log line number. Nothing is summarized away and nothing is invented. It is benchmarked against Drain3 and a raw-logs control on LogHub-2.0 with a blind-judge agent-diagnosis evaluation.

Does ArchBTL replace my LLM?

No. ArchBTL sits in front of your LLM. Send raw logs to ArchBTL, get back just the lines that matter as schema-valid JSON, then pass that to whatever agent you already use: Claude, GPT, Gemini, or a local model.

Which log formats does ArchBTL support?

Anything line-oriented: JSON logs, syslog, framework-specific formats like Hadoop, Spark, HDFS, and Kubernetes, and unstructured application logs. Preprocessing handles parsing and PII redaction before templating.

How much does ArchBTL cost?

ArchBTL is free forever on the Free plan, which includes 50 MB per month. Paid plans start at $19 per month for Hobby and $199 per month plus usage for Team. Pricing is by compute, not seats.
